The story behind CRICKAI.

About the Project

What is CRICKAI?

CRICKAI is an end-to-end, AI-powered cricket analysis platform that uses computer vision and deep learning to evaluate batting and bowling performance from standard smartphone video. Rather than relying on expensive motion-capture labs or manual assessment, CRICKAI delivers automated, real-time analysis accessible to any player or coach with a phone camera.

Architecture

Four tightly-integrated components.

Each module addresses a distinct aspect of cricket performance, combining into a unified analysis pipeline.

01
⚡

Shot Classification

Parses video frames using MediaPipe pose estimation, extracting body-landmark sequences fed into a temporal convolutional network to categorise batting strokes across multiple shot types.

02
⇌

Shot Similarity

Compares a player's pose-landmark trajectories against reference professional players using Dynamic Time Warping and learned embeddings, producing quantitative similarity scores.

03
🎯

Bowler Performance

Tracks ball trajectory via YOLOv8 object detection combined with homography-based pitch mapping to extract speed, length, line, and session-aggregate statistics from video.

04
⚖

Bowling Legality

Estimates elbow extension angles from pose landmarks across the bowling action to detect hyperextension beyond the 15° ICC threshold, flagging potentially illegal actions.

About the Team & SLIIT

Built at SLIIT.

About the Research

Dissertation & Deliverables

This research sits at the intersection of computer vision, deep learning, and sports analytics. Each team member contributes an individual component dissertation focusing on their specific module, alongside a consolidated research paper and an integrated mobile application.

Core Contributions

1. Markerless video-based pose estimation pipeline for cricket motion analysis using MediaPipe.

2. Temporal deep learning model for multi-class batting shot classification from landmark sequences.

3. Biomechanical similarity scoring combining DTW with learned shot embeddings.

4. Real-time ball tracking and pitch-mapping system for bowling performance metrics.

Our Motivation

Cricket coaching and performance analysis today is either subjective — relying on a coach's eye — or prohibitively expensive, requiring specialised motion-capture equipment and controlled environments.

We believe every player deserves access to data-driven feedback. CRICKAI aims to democratise cricket analytics by making AI-powered analysis accessible through nothing more than a smartphone camera.
